About this forecast

This site reproduces Nate Silver's PELE-based tournament forecast for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. Silver publishes the inputs (team ratings, expected goals per match, home-field advantages) on Silver Bulletin, but not the full tournament-wide probabilities. This simulator fills that gap by Monte Carlo-ing the tournament 20,000 times using a model calibrated to match his published match predictions.

How the model works

Two calibrated sub-models, stitched together:

1. Rating-to-expected-goals. Given two teams' PELE and Tilt ratings, plus home-field flags, the model predicts each team's expected goals. Calibrated against 342 of Silver's published match xG values.

2. Expected-goals-to-scoreline. Given expected goals, the model builds a probability distribution over every possible scoreline using a negative-binomial × Dixon-Coles structure. The exact scoreline matters for group-stage tiebreakers (goal difference, goals scored).

The simulator then plays out a full tournament: 72 group matches → 12 group standings → 8 advancing third-place teams → Round of 32 via FIFA's Annex C lookup → standard knockout through the Final. Knockout matches use extra time and penalty shootouts when needed.

Accuracy

End-to-end (team ratings → final W/D/L), our model matches Silver Bulletin's published forecasts to within about 4 percentage points RMS on win probability for the 72 group matches. The biggest gaps are on extreme blowouts where the model form can't quite capture the non-linearity.

Updates

Silver updates his ratings after each round of matches. This forecast will be refreshed daily to track him.
